The 48-Hour Free Profit Snapshot
Your exact process — from first yes to delivered report in 48 hours
Why 48 hours, not one week: "One week free" sounds vague and like a commitment. "48-hour snapshot" sounds fast, expert, and specific. Owners respond to speed. It signals you already know what you are looking for. The free work is the bait — the 48-hour delivery is the hook that makes them trust you fast enough to pay you next.
1
The 30-Minute Data Collection Meeting
You sit with the owner for exactly 30 minutes. You ask for specific documents — not vague questions. You leave with everything you need. You do not need to understand everything in the room. You take it away and analyse it after.
Documents to ask for:
Last 3 months of energy bills (gas + electric)
One month of supplier invoices (any month)
Last month's Deliveroo / Uber Eats payout statement
Business rates bill or most recent demand
Last month's payroll total (rough is fine)
Last month's total revenue (EPOS report or estimate)
Card processing statement (if they have one)
Menu — current printed or online version
2
What You Do in the 48 Hours (with Claude)
You use Claude to build the analysis. You enter the numbers from the documents. Claude helps you identify the gaps against benchmarks, calculate the monthly loss figures, and build the visual report. You do not need specialist software. You need Claude and a spreadsheet.
Your 48-hour analysis checklist:
Calculate actual food cost % from invoices vs revenue
Check energy tariff — is it deemed/rolled-over?
Check business rates against relief eligibility
Calculate delivery margin after commission
Check labour cost % from payroll vs revenue
Menu cost each dish against sale price
Identify 3–5 highest-impact fixes
Build visual profit map using this toolkit
3
The Report Delivery (WhatsApp Voice Note + PDF)
Do not email a PDF cold. Send a WhatsApp voice note first. Say: "Hi [name], I have finished your snapshot. I have found some things I think you will want to see. Can I come in tomorrow at [time] to go through it with you? It will take 20 minutes." Then show up in person with the printed report. The in-person delivery is where you convert free to paid.
At the delivery meeting, say: "Based on what I found in 48 hours — and I have only scratched the surface — you are losing approximately £[X] every month. The full scan takes one day and goes much deeper. Given what I have already found, would it make sense to do the full analysis? My fee is £750. Given what we have already identified, that pays for itself in the first month."
4
If They Say Yes to the Full Scan
Book it immediately. Ask for a date this week. Take a 50% deposit — £375 — before you start. This is normal and professional. Say: "I take a 50% deposit to secure the date and the remaining £375 on delivery of the full report." Anyone who refuses a deposit is unlikely to pay in full later.
5
If They Say Not Yet
Do not push. Say: "Completely fine. Can I leave the report with you? And would it be okay if I checked in with you in two weeks?" Put a reminder in your phone. Follow up exactly when you said you would. Owners respect reliability — it is rare in their world. Most conversions happen on the second or third follow-up, not the first.
Scripts & Objection Handling
For every scenario — including the ones that tripped you up before
Scenario A — Meeting a staff member, not the owner
Turn them into your internal champion
"Hi — are you the manager here? I am a food economist, I work with restaurant owners to find money they are losing without realising. If not, you keep the report and I walk away."
The phrase "you keep the report and I walk away" removes the last objection. There is zero risk to them. If they still say no, leave the document and go — never push twice in the same visit.
